Before being called Korea, Korea was called "Joseon" by the ancestors.
Joseon was ruled by kings, like any other hierarchy. Joseon was also the Golden Age of Korea,
the invention of language, arts, mathematics were all developed during this time of age. More specifically,
once King Sejong, also known as Sejong the Great, took over the throne, he led Korea to their prime, creating
the culture and arts that people enjoy to this day. Joseon was created of 10 districts that the king managed on his own,
so he would usually leave it to his generals and ministers that he entrusted to take care of each districts.

citizens were seperated by class. Higher-class people would serve the king,
or spend their times expanding their powers. Higher-classed people sometimes
took advantage of the citizens, stealing tax money and punishing citizens heavily
if they ever spoke up. Citizens refused to sit quiet, and had revolutions surrounding the districts.
However, there were higher-classed people who chose to help citizens, feeding them, paying them, helping them find jobs,
and looking out for them. Lower-class citizens would work for the king or higher-classes, farm, cook, or run restaurants.
