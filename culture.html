Korea's traditional clothes are called "Hanbok" (한복). Hanbok means "Korea's clothes",
the "Han" meaning Korea and "Bok" meaning clothes. These traditional clothes were worn by Korean ancestors whom invented the clothes.
These Hanbok were made for casual wears back in the day, colorful hanboks worn by higher-classed people, and simple colored clothes were
worn by lower-classed people.

Korea has many traditional foods and snacks. The most well known traditional food being "Bibimbap", where one mixes a variety of vegetables,
meat and egg, along with a spicy pepper paste called "Gochujang". Another traditional food is "Yakgwa", which is a traditional snack
that were commonly enjoyed. It is a deep-fried dough coated in sweet sugar, then dried before enjoyed. It's sweet and oily, a children's favorite snack.
